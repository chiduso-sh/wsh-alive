## The problem with always going hard

Modern fitness culture has a obsession with intensity. Every workout has to be maximum effort, every session needs to leave you gasping. But what if the opposite approach builds stronger, more durable bodies?

## What slow movement actually means

Slow movement isn't about being lazy. It's about deliberate, controlled effort — lifting with tempo, walking with intention, stretching with awareness. It's Zone 2 cardio instead of HIIT. It's a 4-second eccentric on every rep.

## The science backs it up

Studies consistently show that lower-intensity training, done consistently over months and years, produces superior long-term adaptations compared to short bursts of high intensity followed by burnout and injury.

## How to start

1. Replace one HIIT session per week with a 45-minute Zone 2 walk
2. Add a 3-second pause at the bottom of every squat
3. Prioritize sleep over an extra gym session

The goal is to still be training in 30 years.
